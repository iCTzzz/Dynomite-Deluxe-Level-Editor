This is the editor for creating your own new
Stomped puzzle levels with the PopCap game
Dynomite. If you don't have Dynomite, drop by
popcap.com to grab a copy!

NOTE: The Dynomite Editor must be installed in
the same directory as Dynomite is for it to work!

may not be able to help with any bugs or problems
associated with it. It should work pretty good,
though.

=======================
IMPORTANT TERMS TO KNOW
=======================

PUZZLE: A collection of one or more rounds. A round
is completed by destroying all eggs onscreen, letting
you progress to the next round in the puzzle.

ROUND: An individual level in a puzzle.

PUZZLE VIEW: The overview screen that shows you all
the rounds in your puzzle, allowing you to add new
rounds and change the difficulty settings.

ROUND VIEW: The screen for editing rounds. This is where
you'll spend most of your time, adding and removing eggs
and the like to the playfield.

=============================================
HOW TO MAKE A STOMPED PUZZLE IN 23 EASY STEPS
=============================================

1.  Run dynedit.exe.

2.  To start a new puzzle, click NEW PUZZLE on the bottom
    of the screen. Otherwise, the last puzzle you worked on
    will be loaded up.

3.  Click the black square that reads "New Round" to create
    your first puzzle level.

4.  A round icon appears on the screen, labelled "New Round."
    Click on it once, and then select "Round View" on the
    sidebar. You can also just click on a selected round,
    and it'll switch you to the Round View automatically.

5.  Move your cursor over the "Round Properties" bar, and
    double-click it so that it expands.

6.  Click on the Round Name edit box, and enter a name for
    your round (i.e. "The Egg Challenge" or "The First Round!")

7.  Adjust the Eggs Per Stomp number by clicking on the arrows
    to the left or right of the number.  This determines how many
    eggs you can fire before Mama puts her foot down.

8.  Adjust the shaking eggs slider for your personal preference.
    Few will produce almost no Bonus Babies on the level.  Lots
    will ensure that at least one egg is always shaking.

9.  Double-click on the round properties title bar so that it
    shrinks again.

10. Move the cursor over the Walls, and hold down the mouse button
    and drag them until the playfield is at the desired width.

11. Move the cursor over the ceiling (at the top of the screen)
    and click and drag until the ceiling is at the desired height.

12. Move the cursor over slingshot and drag it (left/right) 
    until the slingshot is in the desired position.

13. Click on one of the egg symbols in the sidebar.

14. Click the mouse cursor in the round area to place eggs.
    You can right-click to remove them quickly.

15. Click on a bonus item in the sidebar, and then click on
    an egg in the puzzle in order to set a bonus item on an egg.

16. Double-click on the Launch Queue bar to expand it.

17. Click on egg positions to place eggs in the egg queue. The 
    egg queue determines the order that the player will get
    eggs to shoot when playing, so you can stack it as you like.

18. Click on Gravity. This will cause any unsupported eggs to
    fall off the screen, as they would in the actual game.

19. Click on Puzzle View. Now you can add more rounds if you
    wish.

20. You can drag Round icons about to change their order in
    the game. Drag them onto the Recycle bin to remove them
    from the game.

21. Now, drag the skill tokens onto the appropriate rounds.
    These will determine what level players of a particular
    skill level will start on. For instance, if you dragged
    the Hard token onto level 8, players starting your
    puzzle on Hard difficulty would begin at level 8. The Easy
    icon should be placed on round 1.  If no skill tokens are
    placed, the puzzle will not appear in the Dynomite puzzle
    selector!!  If one or more tokens are not placed, that 
    difficulty will not be available to play in Dynomite.

22. Select Save Puzzle and type a name for your puzzle. You can
    load that puzzle later by clicking on Load Puzzle.

23. Open the Stomped directory and find your puzzle, then copy
    and paste it over to the Stomped folder in your Dynomite directory.
    If you can't find a Stomped folder, make one and copy and
    paste the puzzle over to there. You are ready to start playing!

That covers the basics! Experiment and see what you can come up with!

Good luck!
